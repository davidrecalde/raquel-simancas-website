Nature Catalysis · 2025 DOI 10.1038/s41929-025-01421-8

Hydrostatic-pressure synthesis of small-pore zeolites for selective CO2 reduction.

Simancas R., Kojima M., Yamada T. et al. · Nature Catalysis vol. 8, 320–331.

We report a high-pressure synthesis route that stabilizes a new small-pore zeolite topology with unprecedented selectivity for CO2 hydrogenation to methanol — opening a path toward industrial carbon-neutral chemistry on engineered porous catalysts.

Science · 2024 DOI 10.1126/science.adn8842

Directing aluminum distribution in MFI zeolites for direct methane-to-methanol conversion.

Simancas R., García-Ruiz A., Tanaka H. et al. · Science vol. 384, 1015–1020.

Synthetic control of where aluminum sits inside the MFI framework — and therefore where the active copper sites form — enables a direct CH4→CH3OH route operating at milder conditions than any previously reported system.

J. Am. Chem. Soc. · 2024 DOI 10.1021/jacs.4c00382

A new chiral porous framework via tailored organic structure-directing agents.

Simancas R., Yokoi T., Corma A. et al. · JACS vol. 146, 18 224–18 235.

We demonstrate that bespoke chiral OSDAs imprint their handedness onto the resulting zeolite framework — yielding a chiral microporous material with measurable enantioselectivity in test asymmetric reactions. A first step toward heterogeneous asymmetric catalysis on zeolites.

Nature Communications · 2023 DOI 10.1038/s41467-023-37882-5

Confinement effects on Brønsted acid sites in narrow-pore catalysts for biomass upgrading.

Simancas R., Pérez-Botella E. et al. · Nature Communications vol. 14, 2218.

A quantitative model linking pore geometry to acid-site reactivity in biomass-derived oxygenate upgrading. Confinement is shown to override classical acid-strength descriptors — redefining how to select catalysts for next-generation biorefineries.
