00Approach

Designing matter at the angstrom scale to address industrial chemistry's hardest questions.

Our work bridges fundamental zeolite chemistry — synthesis, structural understanding, active-site engineering — with applied catalysis for CO2 conversion, methane upgrading, biomass valorization and environmental remediation.

We combine controlled crystallization, high-pressure synthesis routes, and advanced in situ characterization. Each project is built around close ties to industrial partners, so discoveries can move toward scale.

01Zeolite design & synthesis

Crystallization, control & new topologies.

How can we direct the formation of a microporous framework, atom by atom? We develop synthesis methodologies — including hydrostatic-pressure routes — to access zeolites whose pore geometry is tuned for the chemistry we need.

The group designs and tests organic structure-directing agents (OSDAs), engineers crystallization kinetics, and uses high-pressure conditions to stabilize otherwise inaccessible frameworks. This unlocks topology-by-design — pores tailored for a target molecule or reaction transition state.

02Catalysis & reaction engineering

Active sites, confinement, performance.

From methanol synthesis to selective hydrogenation and NOx abatement — we tune Brønsted and Lewis sites inside microporous channels to deliver more selective, more efficient, and more durable catalytic processes.

Confinement inside angstrom-scale pores can reshape the energy landscape of a reaction — favoring desired intermediates and suppressing side reactions. We quantify these effects, then translate them into engineered catalysts whose performance matches industrial demands.

03CO2 valorization

Closing the carbon loop.

Turning CO2 from a waste product into a feedstock for chemicals and fuels — on materials engineered for selectivity, stability and scale.

We develop zeolite-supported and bifunctional catalysts for CO2 hydrogenation to methanol, olefins and aromatics. The goal is sustainable processes that integrate with renewable hydrogen and captured CO2 streams — making carbon-neutral chemistry economically viable.

04Environmental applications

Clean water, clean air, sustainable materials.

Wastewater treatment, ammonium removal and adsorption-based separations — zeolites engineered for environmental remediation at municipal and industrial scale.

Beyond catalysis, microporous materials are uniquely suited to selective ion exchange and gas separation. We develop chabazite-type and natural-resource-derived zeolites for ammonium removal from wastewater, heavy-metal capture and SOx/NOx abatement — with cost-aware syntheses that scale.

05Methane & biomass upgrading

From low-value feedstocks to high-value molecules.

Direct conversion of methane and biomass-derived intermediates into platform chemicals and fuels — bypassing energy-intensive intermediates.

Methane-to-methanol, alkane dehydroaromatization, and oxygenate upgrading require active sites that can activate strong C–H bonds without runaway over-oxidation. Our work focuses on stabilizing the right metal centers inside the right pore — and on understanding deactivation pathways so industrial lifetimes become achievable.

Targets
CH₄ → CH₃OH · alkane DHA
Sites
Cu · Fe · Mo confined ions
Output
Stable single-step routes
Partner

06Advanced porous materials

Deep-tech frameworks for the next decade.

Beyond classical zeolites — hybrid frameworks, hierarchical porosity, and chiral materials that bring new functionalities to catalysis, separation and storage.

We explore the frontier of porous-material design: chiral zeolites for asymmetric catalysis, hybrid organic–inorganic frameworks, and hierarchical structures that integrate micro-, meso- and macropores in a single architecture. The aim is to equip industrial chemistry with new tools — not just incremental optimization.
